Do you talk to patients about COVID-19 test results?

The Canadian Digital Service (CDS) is working to help the government’s response to COVID-19. We are conducting research in the upcoming weeks to inform the national COVID app. Particularly, on the healthcare worker experience when accessing and sharing information with people who test positive for COVID-19. The studies will be conducted through phone or video-conference and should take about 15-30 minutes.

How to get involved

If you are interested in volunteering to participate in a study:

  1. Contact Adrianne Lee at cds-snc@tbs-sct.gc.ca to express your interest
  2. We will then ask you some preliminary questions to help us determine how you may qualify for our studies
  3. We may then contact you to participate in a study

The following explains how the Government of Canada will handle your information throughout and following this research session.

By providing your written or verbal consent, you agree to the following:

  • You are volunteering and can stop the session at any time for any reason, without any consequences.
  • The information you provide will be anonymous - which means that your responses will not be linked to you. This also means that you won’t be able to withdraw or correct your feedback once it is provided.
  • We may collect information like general details about your employment, your knowledge about and experience with COVID-19 result notification, and your experience using our website.
  • We won’t note down any information that may come up in the conversation that would identify you (e.g. names of your employer, your address, your name).
  • Your participation and answers won’t affect your access to Government of Canada services or benefits.

What we will collect

By participating in this research, you consent that general details about your employment, your knowledge about and experience with COVID-19 result notification, and your experience using our website may be collected.

How we will use this information

The Canadian Digital Service (CDS) will use this information to help the government’s response to COVID-19.

This personal information will not be used for any “administrative purposes”. In other words, your information will not be used to make a decision that affects you or your access to Government of Canada services.

CDS may publish or share with other government institutions a summary of what we have learned from this research, including quotes or narratives. Your name will not be associated with your responses, quotes, or narratives.

Who we are

CDS is part of the Treasury Board of Canada Secretariat (TBS).

The collection and use of your personal information by TBS is authorized by the Financial Administration Act.

The collection, use, and disclosure of your personal information is in accordance with the federal Privacy Act. Under the Privacy Act, you have a to protection of, access to and correction or notation of your personal information.

The Standard Personal Information Bank entitled “Outreach Activities, PSU 938”, describes the personal information that may be written down.

Your legal rights

Due to the anonymity of responses, CDS will have no reliable means of associating you with your information provided. This means we will not be able to respond to any requests to withdraw your information or correct inaccurate information.

CDS/TBS will avoid recording your personal information. For any questions or concerns, please contact the researcher or their privacy office:
