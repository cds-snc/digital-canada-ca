Help us design a service to report cybercrimes to the Royal Canadian Mounted Police (RCMP)

Have you ever been phished, called by a scammer, or been hacked? Have you ever lost money or data from an online crime?

We’d like to speak to you if you, or a loved-one, have ever experienced an online crime. This is to help with research we’re doing to better understand what Canadians need from a reporting service.

If you would like to help us, or know someone else who can, please contact melissa.banyard@tbs-sct.gc.ca for more information on how to be involved.

How you can help

We'd like to talk to you (via phone, video chat, or in-person) and show you a work in-progress website to get your feedback. The conversation shouldn't take longer than 30 minutes, and we can reach you at a time that's convenient for you.

Your privacy

You're volunteering to take part in this research, so you can choose to stop participating at any time for any reason.

We (the Canadian Digital Service) will make sure your responses are confidential, which means they will not be linked back to anyone.

Your participation and answers will not impact your relationship with the Canadian Digital Service (CDS), the Royal Canadian Mounted Police (RCMP), or any part of the Government of Canada.

We handle all personal information in accordance with the Privacy Act, and we'll give you a copy of our Privacy Statement.
