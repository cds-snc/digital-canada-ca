Accessibility statement

COVID Alert supports common adaptive technologies. It works across the range of phones supported by Google and Apple exposure notification technology, as long as the phone has at least an Android 6 or Apple iOS 13.5 operating system.

Known issues

The Canadian Digital Service (CDS) conducted research, inclusive design reviews and followed best practices for clear language, inclusive design and compatibility with adaptive technology. For general support and questions about COVID Alert, visit the app’s webpage.

You can also call Health Canada and the Public Health Agency of Canada’s COVID-19 information line at 1-833-784-4397.

Accessibility at CDS

CDS is committed to building accessible and inclusive services. We want to meet everyone's needs no matter how they use services. Our goal is to make it better than it was yesterday.

CDS is working to connect with disability communities. We’re testing the app with people with disabilities, including people who use screen readers and people with low vision.

Read our Accessibility Handbook.

Our standard

At a minimum, our services must meet level AA of the Web Content Accessibility Guidelines 2.1 (WCAG 2.1).

We also follow the:

  • Canadian Charter of Rights and Freedoms
  • Bill C-81 Accessible Canada Act
  • Harmonised European Standard, EN 301 549 (2018)
  • Guidelines on making information technology usable by all

Tell us how we're doing

We’re grateful for your feedback and insights. To tell us about accessibility issues in COVID Alert, please do one of the following:

  • Create an issue on our public GitHub repository.
  • Email us at accessibility@cds-snc.ca.
